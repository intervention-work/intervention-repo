What we do

Built for every family.

One method. Four areas of deep specialty.

Substance use

For when nothing else has worked.

Professionally guided interventions for alcohol, opioids, stimulants, and prescription dependency — built around the family, not the addiction.

Mental health

When the mind is the obstacle.

For families navigating depression, anxiety, bipolar disorder, and dual-diagnosis cases. We treat the whole person and their support system together.

Eating disorders

A specialized, evidence-based path.

Eating disorders carry the highest mortality of any mental illness. Our specialists are trained for the precision and tenderness this requires.

Youth & teen

Help that meets adolescents where they are.

For families with a teenage child in crisis. We combine clinical expertise with the developmental sensitivity adolescents need.

How it works

A clear path forward.

Four steps, one continuous relationship.

01

A free, confidential conversation.

We answer in under an hour, 24 hours a day. There is no script. We listen first.

Most families call us mid-crisis. The first call is not a sales pitch — it is a structured intake that captures what is happening, who is involved, and the immediate risks. By the time we hang up, you have a senior interventionist assigned and a tentative plan.

02

A tailored approach for your family.

Your interventionist designs the meeting around the people in the room — not a template.

No two interventions look alike. We map the family system, identify the right model (Johnson, ARISE, SMART, or a hybrid), choose participants, write the letters, and prepare every contingency — including the most common pushback patterns.

03

A structured, loving conversation.

We are in the room. We open the door to help. Every moment is guided.

The meeting itself usually lasts ninety minutes. Your interventionist runs it. We carry the weight so the family can hold the love. By the end, a treatment placement is in motion — often the same day.

04

Ongoing support for the long road.

Recovery is a family project. We stay with you through the first year and beyond.

Family coaching continues for as long as you need it. We coordinate with treatment teams, help navigate setbacks, and meet quarterly to check in. Most families stay in contact for years.
